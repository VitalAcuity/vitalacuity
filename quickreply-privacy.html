Privacy Policy

Quick Reply for Android
Last updated April 20, 2026
Plain English summary: Quick Reply stores your templates locally on your device. We collect no personal data, use no analytics, show no ads, and share nothing with third parties. Ever.
01

Data collection

Quick Reply does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data. All templates, categories, and app preferences are stored exclusively on your device using Android's local database.

No data ever leaves your phone unless you explicitly use the export feature to create a backup file, which goes only where you choose to save it.

02

What stays on your device

  • Template titles, bodies, and categories you create
  • Pin and sort preferences
  • Any variable placeholders you define
  • App settings and preferences
03

Third parties

Quick Reply does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising networks, crash reporting services, or tracking SDKs. There are no ads in the app and no advertiser relationships of any kind.

04

Permissions

Quick Reply requests no special Android permissions. It does not access your contacts, location, camera, microphone, or any other sensitive device feature. The clipboard is only written to when you explicitly tap "Copy to clipboard."

05

Children's privacy

Quick Reply is intended for users aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.

06

Changes to this policy

Any future updates to this privacy policy will be posted at this URL. Continued use of the app after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
