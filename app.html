ACLS Medications

Epinephrine 1 mg IV/IO every 3-5 minutes
Mechanism: Alpha- and beta-adrenergic agonist (vasopressor).
Use: Cardiac arrest (all rhythms) – improves coronary and cerebral perfusion.
Amiodarone 300 mg IV/IO (then 150 mg if needed)
Mechanism: Class III antiarrhythmic.
Use: Refractory ventricular fibrillation / pulseless ventricular tachycardia (VF/pVT).
Alternative: Lidocaine 1–1.5 mg/kg IV/IO.
Lidocaine 1–1.5 mg/kg IV/IO (then 0.5–0.75 mg/kg)
Use: Alternative to amiodarone for refractory VF/pVT.
Magnesium Sulfate 1–2 g IV/IO
Use: Torsades de pointes or suspected hypomagnesemia.
Atropine (no longer routinely recommended for asystole/PEA)
Note: Removed from routine ACLS algorithm in recent guidelines. May still be used for symptomatic bradycardia in specific cases.
Vasopressin (no longer recommended)
Note: Removed from ACLS cardiac arrest algorithm.

PALS Medications

Epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg IV/IO (max 1 mg) q3-5 min
Use: Cardiac arrest and symptomatic bradycardia with poor perfusion.
Amiodarone 5 mg/kg IV/IO (max 300 mg)
Use: Refractory VF/pVT or unstable VT.
Adenosine 0.1 mg/kg rapid IV push (max 6 mg), then 0.2 mg/kg (max 12 mg)
Use: Stable supraventricular tachycardia (SVT).
Atropine 0.02 mg/kg IV (min 0.1 mg)
Use: Symptomatic bradycardia.

NRP Medications

Epinephrine 0.01–0.03 mg/kg IV/IO (0.1–0.3 mL/kg of 1:10,000)
Use: HR <60 bpm despite adequate ventilation and chest compressions.
Notes: Preferred IV/IO route; repeat every 3–5 minutes if needed.
Normal Saline or Blood 10 mL/kg IV/IO
Use: Suspected hypovolemia or acute blood loss.

RSI Drugs

Induction Agents

Etomidate 0.3 mg/kg IV
Mechanism: GABA agonist – minimal hemodynamic effects.
Onset: 30-60 seconds
Duration: 3-8 minutes
Best for: Shock, trauma, unstable patients.
Notes: Single dose only due to adrenal suppression.
Ketamine 1.5-2 mg/kg IV
Mechanism: NMDA antagonist – dissociative anesthetic.
Onset: 45-60 seconds
Duration: 10-20 minutes
Best for: Hypotension, asthma, bronchospasm.
Notes: Maintains blood pressure and airway reflexes.
Propofol 1-2 mg/kg IV
Mechanism: GABA agonist – rapid sedation.
Onset: 30-60 seconds
Duration: 5-10 minutes
Best for: Hemodynamically stable patients.
Notes: Can cause hypotension – reduce dose if unstable.

Paralytics

Succinylcholine 1.5-2 mg/kg IV
Mechanism: Depolarizing blocker – fastest onset.
Onset: 45-60 seconds
Duration: 6-10 minutes
Contraindications: Hyperkalemia, burns >24h, crush injury, malignant hyperthermia, neuromuscular disease.
Rocuronium 1-1.2 mg/kg IV
Mechanism: Non-depolarizing blocker.
Onset: 60-90 seconds
Duration: 30-60 minutes
Notes: Reversible with sugammadex if available.
RSI is a team procedure. Always prepare backup airway devices.
